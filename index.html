Pre-purchase checklist

Convertibles hide their sins in the top and the floors. Work this list before any deposit.

  • Run the VIN through NMVTIS first — free, catches salvage/flood/junk titles even when listing claims "clean." Convertibles get totaled in side hits more often than coupes/sedans — this matters here.
  • Operate the top through a full cycle — twice, from fully closed to fully open and back. Listen for hydraulic groans, motor whining, or fabric binding. A broken power top can be $1,500+.
  • Inspect the headliner and carpet for water stains — even if the top looks clean. Leaky tops drain into the rear footwells; pull up the floor mat and feel for damp padding.
  • Check rear-window seal on fabric tops — plastic windows yellow and crack first. A new top can be $1,000–$2,500.
  • Hardtop convertibles (BMW E46, Chrysler Crossfire, etc.): watch for clicking from the trunk during the roof cycle — failing micro-switches strand the roof half-deployed.
  • Look for chassis rust around the rocker panels and rear-shock mounts — convertibles have less structural rigidity than coupes and rust eats them faster.
  • Demand a Carfax or AutoCheck before any deposit. If the seller refuses, walk away.
  • Get a pre-purchase inspection ($100–$200) from an independent mechanic — non-negotiable on private-party convertibles. Many shops have specialists for specific marques (Miata, BMW, Porsche).
